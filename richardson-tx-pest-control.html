How To Prevent Termite Damage In Richardson, TX

Termites are widely known for their ability to chew through the wooden structure of your Richardson home or business, causing significant damage in the process. They live in large underground nests with colonies that can contain anywhere from 60,000 to over one million individual termites. Unfortunately, these destructive pests thrive in Richardson’s warm weather, making them a year-round threat. The key to preventing termites from gaining access to your home or business is to remove the things that may attract them to your property in the first place. Keep termites away from your property by implementing the termite prevention tips listed below:

  • Get rid of wooden materials that could attract termites, such as logs, stumps, and fallen trees.

  • Replace landscaping mulch with a non-wooden alternative, such as crushed rock.

  • Keep direct wood-to-soil contact to a minimum by maintaining an 18-inch gap between the soil and wooden portions of your property.

  • Minimize sources of excess moisture by removing water-damaged wood, installing dehumidifiers, and repairing leaking pipes.

  • Clear debris out of gutters and ensure downspouts divert water away from your foundation.

  • Remove access to your property by sealing gaps and crevices in your foundation and exterior walls.

Signs Bed Bugs Leave Behind In Richardson, TX

Bed bugs are irritating pests that have flat, oval bodies and a reddish-brown appearance. Although bed bugs are not dangerous pests, their blood-feeding behavior and strong instincts make them incredibly difficult to control and prevent. Bed bugs typically hide during the day and can squeeze into tight gaps and crevices in your furniture, behind wallpaper, and under floorboards. Once nighttime arrives, bed bugs emerge from their hiding spaces to feed on your blood as you sleep. Preventing bed bugs from getting into your Richardson property is challenging, and getting rid of them once they invade is even more so. When it comes to dealing with a bed bug infestation, the sooner you identify their presence, the sooner you can reach out for professional bed bug control services. Identify bed bugs by keeping an eye out for the following signs:

  • Small spots of reddish-brown fecal matter on your bedding and sheets.

  • Bloodstains on your pillowcase, clothing, and blankets.

  • Shed skins, tiny bed bug eggs, or adult bed bugs.

  • An unpleasant musty odor that becomes stronger as the bed bug infestation worsens.

  • Rows or clusters of itchy red bites on your skin.
